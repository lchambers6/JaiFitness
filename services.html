Private Yoga

JAI Fitness Pro offers a variety of yoga formats giving clients a well-rounded practice that will not only strengthen and stretch the muscular body but will allow restoration and a greater sense of control over a chaotic mind. Traditionally, the purpose of asanas in yoga was to prepare for hours-long seated meditation. Now, in the US, the purpose of asana has expanded and includes to build strength, develop focus, find balance, increase flexibility, and allow rest. Based on your goals and interests, your sessions will consist of vinyasa, yin, restorative, or a combo of the three.

Vinyasa - A vinyasa yoga practice coordinates breath with movement. Sessions will focus on building eccentric and isometric strength which creates a strong foundation for participation in other demanding activities such as hiking, weight lifting, rock climbing, etc. Vinyasa yoga can be practiced in a heated room or at room temperature.

Yin - A yin yoga practice focuses on gaining flexibility in the muscles and connective tissue, therefore safely increasing range of motion throughout the body. This deep stretch format requires holding each posture for 3-7 minutes with the usage of props as necessary. Yin can be practiced in a heated room or at room temperature.

Restorative - The purpose of restorative yoga is to rest. Contrary to popular belief, it is very different from yin yoga. Minimal stretching is involved with restorative yoga. Multiple props are required - blankets, bolsters, blocks, a strap, sandbags, etc. There are significant physical and mental benefits resulting from a regular restorative practice. Restorative yoga should be done at room temperature.
